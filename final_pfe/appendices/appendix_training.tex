\chapter{Whisper-Small Fine-Tuning for Warsh}
\label{app:training}

We trained \texttt{benhadjermed/tahkik-small-warsh} by fine-tuning
\texttt{openai/whisper-small} on the pre-processed Warsh dataset
\texttt{benhadjermed/warsh\_quran\_aligned\_processed} (three reciters,
${\sim}13{,}000$ aligned ayah segments). The dataset was assembled by
the preparation notebooks in \texttt{project-L3/} (\texttt{tahkik\_01\_prepare\_dataset.ipynb}
plus the \texttt{tahkik\_dataset\_v2\_*} series for download,
normalisation, and feature extraction). Training itself runs in
\texttt{tahkik\_small\_warsh\_train.ipynb} on Google Colab Pro
(NVIDIA T4 / A100), with checkpoints saved every 250 steps to Google
Drive so a disconnected session can be resumed transparently. The
resulting checkpoint is published to our private model registry and
consumed by the inference server documented in Appendix~\ref{app:server}.

\section{Dataset and result}
\label{sec:app_training_result}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Dataset:} \texttt{benhadjermed/warsh\_quran\_aligned\_processed}
        --- Warsh recitations from three reciters, audio resampled to
        16\,kHz mono, text normalised with Warsh-specific orthographic
        fixes, log-Mel features pre-computed and labels tokenised so
        the training notebook does no heavy preprocessing.
  \item \textbf{Outcome:} the multi-reciter training run reduces the
        validation Word Error Rate from $34.62\%$ down to
        $\mathbf{6.00\%}$ on the held-out Warsh test split, lowest at
        the step-3{,}000 checkpoint. That checkpoint is the one
        published as \texttt{benhadjermed/tahkik-small-warsh} and
        loaded by the inference server.
\end{itemize}
